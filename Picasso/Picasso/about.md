# About

“Reality is Data, Art is the Interface.”


Quentin “Jack” Cook is an American contemporary digital artist working at the intersection of machine learning, generative systems, and collectible media. Known for translating high dimensional data into immersive visual experiences, Cook’s work explores how intelligence can be rendered, felt, and owned. 

Raised in the United States, Cook developed an early fascination with both mathematics and visual expression. His artistic instincts were shaped as much by abstract theory as by aesthetic movements. Rather than imitating past styles, he became interested in building systems that could produce entirely new ones. This led him to experiment with neural networks, embedding spaces, and synthetic data as raw artistic mediums.

Cook’s practice centers on constructing pipelines where models interpret, transform, and generate visual artifacts from latent representations. His work often begins with large datasets which are encoded into high dimensional embeddings. From there, he designs generative systems that traverse these spaces, producing fluid, evolving outputs that feel both organic and computational. The result is a body of work that exists somewhere between painting, simulation, and signal.

Cook’s work is driven by a central question: what does it mean to see through the eyes of a model? His pieces often evoke motion through latent space interpolations, distortions, and emergent structures that feel simultaneously familiar and alien. This tension is intentional, reflecting a world increasingly mediated by machine perception.
